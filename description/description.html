Causes of Floods

Floods can be caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • Heavy Rainfall: Prolonged or intense rain can overwhelm drainage systems.
  • Storm Surges: Coastal areas can experience flooding due to high winds and storm surges.
  • Snowmelt: Rapid melting of snow can lead to increased river flow.
  • Dam Failure: The breaking of dams can cause sudden and severe flooding.
  • Urbanization: Increased development can reduce natural drainage and increase runoff.

Precautions and Safety Measures

To stay safe during a flood, consider the following precautions:

  • Move to higher ground if you are in a flood-prone area.
  • Stay away from floodwaters as they may be contaminated or swift-moving.
  • Do not drive through flooded roads; you cannot judge the depth of the water.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials like food, water, and medications.
